chatwire

Your messages, your server, your rules.
Open-source iMessage bridge. Access your messages from any device — your phone, your laptop, your browser. One command to install, zero servers to manage.

pipx install chatwire

Web UI

A full React interface for reading and sending iMessages. 23 themes, full-text search, attachments, group chats, tapback reactions, and read receipts — everything you'd expect from a modern chat app. Installable as a PWA on desktop and mobile.

Telegram Relay

Reply to your iMessages from Telegram. Full two-way bridge with contact names, group support, and delivery confirmation. Your iMessages and Telegram messages stay in sync — reply from whichever app you have open.

Plugin System

Extend chatwire with ntfy push notifications, MQTT, Home Assistant, XMPP, and more. Every plugin gets an auto-generated settings section in the web UI. Or build your own — the SDK is open and community plugins are welcome.

Automation Rules

A condition/action rules engine fires on every message. Write rules in a human-readable DSL: from:Alice AND contains:urgent. Actions include webhook, suppress, log, and auto-reply. Rules live in a JSON file and are fully editable from the Settings UI.

MCP Integration

Connect AI agents to your messages. 10 tools, 4 permission scopes, and a confirmation flow that keeps you in control. Claude, ChatGPT, and local LLMs supported. The protocol is open — build your own MCP client or use any compatible one.

Zero Telemetry

No analytics. No crash reporting. No "anonymized usage data." Your messages stay on your Mac. We don't know how many users we have, and we're fine with that. If you want to help us improve, open an issue on GitHub.

How it works

1

Install on your Mac

Run pipx install chatwire. The setup wizard walks you through granting Full Disk Access and Automation permissions — two macOS prompts, nothing more. Runs on macOS 12 Monterey and up.

2

Your Mac reads chat.db

chatwire watches ~/Library/Messages/chat.db with your explicit permission. It resolves senders against Contacts.app and drives Messages.app via AppleScript to send on your behalf. No protocol reverse-engineering. No Apple gray area.

3

Access from anywhere

Browser, phone, Telegram, AI agent — connect from any device. Your messages never touch our servers, because we don't have any. Local network, Tailscale, or Cloudflare Access: you control the perimeter.

Install

chatwire runs on macOS 12 Monterey and later. You need a Mac with your Apple ID logged into Messages.app. Run chatwire doctor to verify your system is ready.

Recommended — pipx

pipx installs chatwire into an isolated environment so it never conflicts with your other Python tools.

pipx install chatwire
chatwire install-agents
chatwire setup

Homebrew

Convenient if you already use brew. Tap source at allenbina/homebrew-tap.

brew install allenbina/tap/chatwire
chatwire install-agents
chatwire setup

curl (no package manager)

No PyPI access, no Homebrew — just a shell. Pin to a specific version with CHATWIRE_REF=v1.1.0.

curl -fsSL https://raw.githubusercontent.com/allenbina/chatwire/main/scripts/install.sh | bash

What we chose, and what we didn't

Bridges to open platforms

We connect to Telegram (open API), Matrix (open protocol), and XMPP (open standard). We don't connect to platforms that prohibit third-party access in their Terms of Service. This isn't a technical limitation — it's a choice.

Every message is human-triggered

We don't offer message scheduling. We don't offer auto-replies. We don't offer "AI that texts for you." Texting is personal. If you're not present enough to send a message yourself, maybe the message doesn't need to be sent.

Zero telemetry. Not "anonymized." Zero.

chatwire doesn't phone home. No analytics, no crash reporting, no usage data. Your messages stay on your Mac. Your reading habits stay on your Mac. We don't know how many users we have, and we're fine with that.

Plugins are a choice, not a mandate

The core app — reading and sending your messages — works with zero plugins installed. We built the plugin system so you can extend chatwire how you want. Not so we can upsell you. Not so we can build a marketplace that takes a cut.

Self-hosted means self-owned

Your chatwire instance runs on your Mac. Your messages never touch our servers — because we don't have servers. There's no cloud component. There's no account to create. There's no subscription. If we disappear tomorrow, your instance keeps running.

The story nobody else has

Chatwire's development — from proof of concept to production — was built through an autonomous AI development loop. Eight phases, 48 commits, full plugin SDK, mobile app scaffold, CI/CD pipeline — built in a single afternoon. The developer approved each phase from his phone while running errands.

This isn't a gimmick. It's a proof of what's possible when you combine clear architectural decisions with autonomous AI execution. The human set the direction, made the judgment calls, and rejected the ideas that didn't align with the project's values: no message scheduling, no WhatsApp bridge, no telemetry. The AI wrote the code.

The entire development history is public. Every commit, every decision, every "no" — it's all in the repo.
